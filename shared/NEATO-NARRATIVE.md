# NEATO NARRATIVE — Positioning Bible

> Canonical reference for all external content. If it's not in here, don't say it.

---

## 1. One-Liner

Neato is a 2P eCommerce accelerator that buys inventory from brands and operates as their exclusive online retailer across every major channel — Amazon, TikTok Shop, DTC, Walmart — fulfilled from a single inventory pool.

---

## 2. Elevator Pitch

Most brands either sell on Amazon themselves (and drown in operational complexity) or hire a 3P agency (and keep all the risk). Neato does neither. We buy your inventory at wholesale, become your exclusive online retailer, and run the same buy-and-sell playbook across every channel — Amazon, TikTok Shop, DTC, Walmart — all fulfilled through a single inventory pool. We make money when we sell. If we don't sell, we eat it. That's the alignment most partners can't offer.

---

## 3. Positioning Statement

**For** established brands doing $10M+ that want to grow online without building an in-house eCommerce team or outsourcing to an agency that doesn't share their risk.

**Neato is** a 2P eCommerce accelerator.

**That unlike** 3P agencies (who take a fee whether you grow or not) and other 2P operators (who only run Amazon or spread teams across 100+ brands),

**Delivers** true omnichannel 2P operations — buying and selling your inventory across every major marketplace from a single fulfillment pool — with a dedicated senior team and proprietary tech built to run the entire operation.

---

## 4. Core Differentiators

### Omnichannel 2P
Every other 2P operator runs Amazon. Neato runs the 2P model on every channel — Amazon, TikTok Shop, DTC/Shopify, Walmart. Same playbook: buy inventory, sell it online, own the customer experience. The difference is fulfillment. Amazon's Multi-Channel Fulfillment means one inventory pool serves every channel. No split stock. No separate 3PLs per channel. No channel-specific ops teams. One operation, everywhere.
*This is the vision Neato is actively building toward as TikTok Shop and DTC programs launch. No competitor is attempting this.*

### Dedicated Senior Teams
The largest operators manage 100+ brands. That means your brand gets a junior account manager following a playbook. Neato keeps its portfolio intentionally concentrated — every partner gets a dedicated senior team with direct access to leadership. Your strategy isn't templated. It's built by the people who built the company.

### Speed
No committee approvals. No quarterly review cycles. Pricing changes, creative pivots, channel launches happen in days, not quarters. When a competitor makes a move or a trend breaks, Neato reacts at founder speed. This is a structural advantage of the operating model, not a promise.

### Proprietary Tech Stack
Three systems built in-house: **Impact** (analytics and marketplace visibility), **Flow** (Amazon prep operations + demand forecasting), **Basecamp** (unified OS combining work management, Flow/Impact data, and automation/AI). These aren't dashboards bolted onto third-party tools. They're the operating system Neato runs on — and they give brands visibility they can't get anywhere else.

### Skin in the Game
Neato buys inventory. If it doesn't sell, Neato loses money — not the brand. This is fundamentally different from agency models (where the agency gets paid regardless) and aggregator models (where the aggregator owned the brand outright). Every decision Neato makes is backed by its own capital. Incentives are perfectly aligned.

## 5. Competitor Framing Rules

### Pattern ($2.6B IPO, 100+ brands)
- **Say:** Pattern validated the 2P model at scale. They proved this works. They're also 94% Amazon-dependent and manage 100+ brands with templated operations.
- **Don't say:** Pattern is bad, failing, or irrelevant. They're a public company. Respect the scale.
- **Frame:** "They proved 2P works on Amazon. We're building 2P for every channel."

### Spreetail ($1B+ GMV)
- **Say:** Spreetail specializes in oversized and hard-to-ship products. Different category focus.
- **Don't say:** Anything competitive. Different lane.
- **Frame:** Acknowledge, move on.

### Recom (Top-10 Amazon seller)
- **Say:** Closest competitor by model on the Amazon side. Health/wellness focused. Serious operator.
- **Don't say:** Anything speculative. They're opaque — don't fill gaps with assumptions.
- **Frame:** Respect the operator. Differentiate on omnichannel vision and tech transparency.

### Luminize (12th largest Amazon seller, ~$240M)
- **Say:** Closest competitor to Neato on the Amazon side. Real operator, serious scale. Natural products, supplements, pets, beauty, grocery.
- **Don't say:** Anything dismissive. They are a legitimate, scaled competitor.
- **Frame:** Similar DNA on Amazon. Differentiate on omnichannel 2P vision, proprietary tech, and multi-channel expansion.

### Front Row (PE-backed, beauty focus)
- **Say:** Strong in beauty (Rare Beauty, Summer Fridays). Backed by Charlesbank and HighPost Capital.
- **Don't say:** They're limited or weak. PE backing means resources.
- **Frame:** Category specialist vs. Neato's cross-category, omnichannel model.

### Aggregators (Thrasio, etc.)
- **Say:** Different model entirely. Aggregators bought brands FROM founders. Neato buys inventory FOR brands. $15B raised collectively — most went bankrupt. Not comparable.
- **Don't say:** "We're not an aggregator" defensively. State the distinction once, clearly, and move on.

## 6. Category Language

| ✅ Use | ❌ Don't Use |
|--------|-------------|
| 2P eCommerce accelerator | Distributor |
| Exclusive online retail partner | Aggregator (ever) |
| Omnichannel 2P operator | 3PL / logistics provider / fulfillment company |
| Brand partner | Client / customer |
| Full-stack marketplace services | End-to-end solutions |
| Multi-channel fulfillment | MCF (externally — too jargony for brands) |

**"2P" is a category descriptor, not a differentiator.** Pattern, Spreetail, Recom, Front Row, and Luminize all do 2P. It's table stakes. The differentiator is running 2P across every channel with unified fulfillment.

## 7. Voice & Tone

**Rule:** If it sounds like it could come from any company's website, rewrite it.

| ❌ Bad | ✅ Good |
|--------|---------|
| "Neato provides innovative eCommerce solutions for brands seeking to maximize their online potential." | "Most brands are either overpaying an agency that doesn't share their risk, or drowning in Amazon ops. We fix that." |
| "Our best-in-class technology platform delivers actionable insights." | "We built three internal tools because nothing on the market did what we needed." |
| "Leveraging our deep expertise across the eCommerce landscape..." | Just don't. |

**Principles:**
1. **Lead with the problem.** What's broken in the brand's current situation? Start there.
2. **Be specific.** Numbers > adjectives. Facts > claims.
3. **Boring = death.** Even in B2B. Especially in B2B.
4. **Earn the claim.** Don't say "best" — show the proof and let them conclude it.
5. **Talk like a person.** Contractions. Short sentences. No jargon walls.

---

## 8. Anti-Patterns

Never use in Neato content:
- ❌ "Innovative solutions" / "cutting-edge" / "best-in-class"
- ❌ "Synergy" / "leverage" (as verb) / "disrupt"
- ❌ Claiming 2P is a differentiator — five other companies do it
- ❌ "We're not an aggregator" as a lead
- ❌ Generic capability lists without proof points
- ❌ Unsubstantiated superlatives ("the leading," "the fastest-growing")
- ❌ Citing specific brand count — never say the number of brands in the portfolio
- ❌ Positioning Neato as a logistics or fulfillment company
- ❌ Describing MCF as a fulfillment service — it's the backbone of omnichannel 2P operations
- ❌ Any sentence that could describe any other company in the space without modification
